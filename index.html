Globglogabgalab

by: Mitchell Oeth

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I came up with a basic idea of a flimsy man that the user controls to whack projectiles away. I slowly built on my ideas while programming the game and ended up with the finished product I have now. I don't really know why I made this game and not a different type of game. I could've gotten other inspiration just as easily and made a completely different game. But I had an idea and decided to stick to it.

2. What I Learned

From user testing, I learned how to improve my game in ways I would not have come up with on my own. I was constantly asking and receiving feedback from my peers and teachers on how I could improve my game. I found that it is extremely helpful to have people making suggestions about my game. I have changed and reworked my game countless times because of helpful feedback.

3. Architecture of My Game

The user controls a flimsy man called glob. All of the projectiles fall from the top of the screen. The user tries to read as many books as possible so that the green bar to the right of the screen fills up to the top. Any hit and missed books raise and lower the bar, respectively. If the bar fills to the top, the player wins. If the bar gets to the bottom, they lose. They try to avoid the bookmarks(which make you stop reading), and get the glasses(which make them invulnerable to bookmarks for 3 seconds. If the player hits three bookmarks, they lose. The Globglogabgalab class starts the game; the GamePanel class looks for player inputs like moving/clicking the mouse and updates/draws the menu, game, and end states; the ObjectManager class manages all the objects by adding, deleting, updating, and drawing them; the GameObject class sets a template for all the objects to follow; and all the object classes do their thing.

4. Technical Implementation

My favorite part of my game is definitely creating glob and how he moves. It took three months to figure out how he moves! I started out by giving him a set track that he would go on in the shape of a half circle as the player moves the mouse from left to right. It was hard to make him move flimsily, as I could only make him move like a dial. With a lot of help from other people, we came up with a lot of different strategies for making him flimsy. After many weeks we came up with this: As glob updates, his circles update starting at the top and ending at the bottom. Each circle takes the circles above and below it, and set its x/y to the average of the two. This creates a delayed effect that looks really smooth.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

If I were to continue working on my game, I would improve it by telling the player how many words they read; I would implement game music and sound effects that I would make myself in Pro Tools; I could create a shop, with all sorts of things to buy: skins, upgrading power-ups, etc. I would also come up with more ideas along the way.
